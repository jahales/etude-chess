# 0013 — v0.1.0 is a play-vs-Maia coaching loop; Maia runs client-side

**Status:** Superseded in timing by ADR [0014](0014-v0.1.0-guess-the-move.md) · 2026-07-17
**What survives:** everything except the release slot — the play-vs-Maia coaching loop described
here ships as **v0.2.0**, unchanged in design. Client-side Maia, the coaching core, and the
deferred-intervention call all stand.

## Context
ADR [0011](0011-game-review-first.md) established that the value is in **coached review**, not
puzzles, and framed v0.1.0's game source as **importing** the user's online games. Two things
then updated that:
1. The owner's framing sharpened: the product is really a **personal coach** — play a human-like
   opponent, and get coached on where the game turned (openings, middlegame, endgame), building
   toward analytics and concept recommendations.
2. Research ([../research/engines.md](../research/engines.md)) confirmed **Maia runs fully
   client-side in the browser** (ONNX via onnxruntime-web — proven by `play-lc0` and the Maia
   Platform frontend). So "play a human-like opponent" needs **no backend** and keeps our
   local-first, static-app architecture.

The owner chose **play-vs-Maia + coach** for v0.1.0 (over import-and-coach or paste-a-PGN).

## Decision
**v0.1.0 = play one game against client-side Maia at your level, then a coached, retrieval-first
review of the 2–3 moments that decided it, plus a by-phase leak summary.** Maia becomes a
**v0.1.0** component (client-side), no longer Phase-3-deferred.

## Why this is coherent, not scope creep
- **The coaching core is identical** regardless of where the game comes from (played vs. imported)
  — same Stockfish grading, fact bundle, decisive-moment detection, commit-before-reveal UI. So
  this reuses, not discards, the ADR-0011 plan; **import becomes a later alternate source.**
- **Playing is the material generator, review is the value** — consistent with Southwick 2026 as
  long as the review is the star and playing is the feeder (constitution: honest about the ceiling).
- **No backend required** — client-side Maia preserves ADR [0009](0009-tech-stack.md).
- Builds the **Maia opponent** once, which later **play-it-out**/sparring (ADR
  [0006](0006-maia-opponent-stockfish-referee.md)) reuses.

## Consequences
- **Maia is pulled forward to v0.1.0** (client-side ONNX). Its delivery — leela2onnx conversion,
  the known float64 fix, model caching, WebGPU/WASM backend — is the release's **biggest build
  risk**; prototype it first (plan M2). Updates ADR [0006](0006-maia-opponent-stockfish-referee.md)'s
  timing (Maia is now v0.1.0, not Phase 3).
- **Two GPL engine artifacts ship** in v0.1.0 (Stockfish WASM + Maia-1 ONNX weights), both
  arm's-length in Workers, both needing license + source-link notices. Client-side Maia uses
  **Maia-1 (GPL)** because Maia-2 (MIT) has no browser path.
- The **mid-game "step back and analyze"** intervention the owner floated is **deferred to v0.2**
  — v0.1.0 stays play-then-review to preserve real-game conditions (interrupting is more
  lesson-like). 
- **Game import is deferred**; the "which platform do you play on" question is parked.
- Supersedes ADR [0011](0011-game-review-first.md)'s *game-source* (import → play-vs-Maia); 0011's
  review-first thesis stands unchanged.
