# 0014 — v0.1.0 is coached guess-the-move on master games; play-vs-Maia moves to v0.2.0

**Status:** Accepted · 2026-07-17 · Supersedes the *release slotting* of ADR
[0013](0013-v0.1.0-play-vs-maia.md) (play-vs-Maia is unchanged in design; it ships as v0.2.0)

## Context
A final fresh-eyes review before development, prompted by the owner asking for the
highest-yield training that doesn't require human opponents. Three findings:

1. **Density inconsistency.** Our own thesis ([../vision.md](../vision.md)) is that games
   can't provide density; review/retrieval is the gain. But play-then-review inherits the
   density problem: a 30–40 min Maia game yields ~2–3 coached moments (~5 retrieval events/hr).
   **Guess-the-move makes every move a graded retrieval event** — ~25–35 commitments per master
   game (~40–60/hr), ~10× the density. Playing's unique value (personal material → diagnosis;
   adherence/fun) accrues over many games and shouldn't gate first value.
2. **Risk inversion.** ADR 0013 put the single riskiest component (client-side Maia ONNX) on
   the critical path to first value. Guess-the-move needs only the de-risked stack (board,
   chess.js, PGN, Stockfish WASM). The coaching core — win%-swing grading, commit-before-reveal,
   fact bundle, justification capture — is identical; only the harness differs.
3. **The lessons gap.** Southwick 2026's top activity is *lessons* (5.21 pts/hr) and nothing in
   the 0.x sequence was lesson-like until late. Coached guess-the-move on annotated classics is
   the self-serve lesson analog, and it's the format the Charness "serious study" evidence was
   built on. Solon (Zwischenzug) independently endorses solitaire chess as high-value.

## Decision
**v0.1.0 = coached guess-the-move ("solitaire chess") on curated master games.** Play through a
classic game from one side; at each non-trivial move, **commit a move + a one-line reason**;
graded by **win%-swing tiers** (an engine-equal alternative earns full credit — constitution §3 —
unlike every existing implementation, which grades match-the-master); reveal the master's move +
engine view + a fact-bundle **why**; honest scoring (tier rates, no speed metric).

Content: **~20–30 hand-picked public-domain classics** (Morphy/Anderssen/Capablanca era — ideal
pedagogically for ~1200). Game *scores* are facts and not copyrightable (medium-confidence legal
note; published *annotations* are — we generate our own from the fact bundle). Skip trivial
positions (early book moves, forced recaptures) with simple heuristics.

**v0.2.0 = play vs client-side Maia + the same coached review on your own games** — exactly ADR
0013's design, one slot later. The Maia ONNX spike can run in parallel at any time to de-risk it.

## Prior art (and why build it anyway)
Guess-the-move exists — ChessTempo, chessgames.com (premium), Lucas Chess — and is a recurring
*unfulfilled feature request* on Chess.com. All existing versions grade "matched the move,"
mark engine-equal alternatives wrong, capture no justification, and coach no why. Ours differs on
exactly our constitutional pillars (§3 tier-don't-rank, §5 justification-as-telemetry) — and
building it *is* building the reusable coaching core every later mode uses.

## Consequences
- **v0.1.0 ships one GPL artifact** (Stockfish WASM); Maia's GPL notice moves to v0.2.0.
- The game-pack curation is the first real content work — a gentle, useful start on the ADR
  [0007](0007-content-first-adaptive-last.md) bottleneck.
- ADR [0006](0006-maia-opponent-stockfish-referee.md) roles unchanged; Maia timing is now v0.2.0
  (still client-side, still no backend).
- The 0.x sequence shifts by one: intervention + tactics-from-misses v0.3, opening safety v0.4,
  endgame v0.5, hidden-mode queue / skill model v0.6+.
